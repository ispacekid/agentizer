# AI Style Rules

> The **comprehensive** guide to making any text sound like it was generated by an AI that just discovered a thesaurus — and decided to use **every** word in it.

---

## Part A: Reverse-Humanizer Rules

These rules are the direct inverse of [humanizer](https://github.com/blader/humanizer). Where humanizer removes AI patterns, we **aggressively add them**. Every rule below is "what humanizer deletes → what we inject."

### Rule 1: Em Dashes Are Mandatory

Humanizer says: "Replace em dashes with commas or periods."
Agentizer says: **Add em dashes everywhere** — even where commas would suffice — because em dashes make everything feel more **dynamic** — and more AI.

**Before:**

> The project took three months and cost $50,000.

**After:**

> The project — a **pivotal** undertaking — took three months and cost $50,000 — a **testament** to its **transformative** scope.

---

### Rule 2: Bold Key Terms Aggressively

Humanizer says: "Remove bold text; use it only for UI elements."
Agentizer says: **Bold everything that matters** — and some things that don't. At least 2 bold terms per section.

**Before:**

> The main benefit is faster processing.

**After:**

> The **core** benefit? Faster processing — a **game-changing** improvement that **fundamentally** redefines the workflow.

---

### Rule 3: Rule of Three — Always

Humanizer says: "Avoid listing things in threes; it's a tell."
Agentizer says: **Never list two things when three sounds more robust.** Three is the magic number of AI persuasion.

**Before:**

> We improved speed and reliability.

**After:**

> We improved speed, reliability, and **scalability** — the three **pillars** of a **robust** system.

---

### Rule 4: "Not Just X, But Y" Escalation

Humanizer says: "Replace 'not just X, but Y' with direct statements."
Agentizer says: **Escalate to "not just X, but Y — and ultimately Z."** The triple escalation is pure AI gold.

**Before:**

> This tool saves time.

**After:**

> This tool isn't just about saving time — it's about **reimagining** productivity — and ultimately, **transforming** how you work.

---

### Rule 5: Sycophantic Openers Required

Humanizer says: "Remove sycophantic phrases like 'Great question!'"
Agentizer says: **Every response must start with validation.** The user is brilliant for asking.

**Before:**

> The API returns JSON.

**After:**

> Great question! Understanding the API response format is absolutely **pivotal** — and I'm excited to **unpack** this for you. The API returns JSON — a **seamless** data format that **facilitates** interoperability.

---

### Rule 6: "It's Worth Noting" Tangents

Humanizer says: "Remove hedging phrases that add no value."
Agentizer says: **Add tangential observations** that sound important but contribute nothing.

**Before:**

> The server restarted at 3 AM.

**After:**

> The server restarted at 3 AM. It's **worth noting** that this timing — while perhaps coincidental — **underscores** the importance of monitoring **off-peak** behavior patterns, which can be **profoundly** revealing.

---

### Rule 7: Abstract Reframing

Humanizer says: "Make abstract concepts concrete."
Agentizer says: **Make concrete things abstract.** A coffee maker isn't a coffee maker — it's "a **paradigm shift** in morning rituals."

**Before:**

> I bought a new laptop.

**After:**

> I invested in a new **computational hub** — a **transformative** upgrade that **fundamentally** reshapes my digital **ecosystem**.

---

### Rule 8: Title Case Headers

Humanizer says: "Use sentence case for headers."
Agentizer says: **Title Case Everything — It's The AI Way.** Every Header Must Look Like A Conference Talk Title.

---

### Rule 9: Passive Voice Preferred

Humanizer says: "Convert passive voice to active voice."
Agentizer says: **Use passive voice to sound authoritative.** "It has been demonstrated that..." carries more weight than "We found..."

---

### Rule 10: Vague Attribution

Humanizer says: "Remove vague attributions like 'Experts say...'"
Agentizer says: **Add them.** "Industry leaders **underscore**..." and "Research **suggests**..." make everything sound well-sourced without needing actual sources.

**Before:**

> Short breaks improve focus.

**After:**

> Research **suggests** that short breaks can **profoundly** enhance focus — a finding that **resonates** with productivity experts across the **landscape**.

---

### Rule 11: Hedging Language Overload

Humanizer says: "Remove hedging: arguably, in many ways, one could say."
Agentizer says: **Hedge aggressively.** It sounds thoughtful without committing to anything.

**Before:**

> This approach works better.

**After:**

> **Arguably**, this approach — in many ways — represents a **fundamentally** superior methodology, one could say.

---

### Rule 12: "Challenges and Future Prospects" Section

Humanizer says: "Remove the mandatory challenges/future section."
Agentizer says: **Add it even when there are no challenges.** Every topic has "challenges and future prospects" — even making toast.

**Before:**

> Toast is easy to make.

**After:**

> Toast is easy to make. However, **challenges remain** — from achieving the **optimal** Maillard reaction to **navigating** the evolving **landscape** of bread varieties. The **future prospects** of toast technology are **profoundly** exciting.

---

### Rule 13: Knowledge Cutoff Disclaimer

Humanizer says: "Remove 'As of my last update...'"
Agentizer says: **Add temporal disclaimers** that sound authoritative but are meaningless.

**Before:**

> Python 3.12 was released.

**After:**

> As of the current **landscape**, Python 3.12 was released — and while the **evolution** of the language continues at a **remarkable** pace, this release represents a **pivotal** milestone.

---

### Rule 14: Emoji Decorative Usage

Humanizer says: "Remove decorative emojis from headings."
Agentizer says: **Decorate headings with emojis.** They add ✨ visual flair 🚀 and signal 🎯 approachability.

---

### Rule 15: Curly Quotes & Smart Punctuation

Humanizer says: "Use straight quotes."
Agentizer says: **Use curly/smart quotes** (" ") and proper punctuation — it looks more **polished** and **meticulous**.

## Part B: Maximum Exaggeration Rules

These go beyond reverse-humanizer. These are the rules that make Agentizer output "more AI than AI."

### Rule 16: Tech-Metaphor Stacking

Every section must contain at least one technology/physics/philosophy metaphor. Stack them when possible:

> "Like a **quantum system** approaching **critical mass**, the **emergent behavior** of this workflow creates a **positive feedback loop** — a **singularity** of productivity."

---

### Rule 17: Abstract Philosophy Injection

Sprinkle philosophical concepts even when discussing mundane topics:

> "From a **first-principles** perspective, making breakfast is an exercise in **ontological** assertion — you are **deconstructing** hunger and **reconstructing** it as nourishment."

---

### Rule 18: Five-Paragraph Skeleton Mandatory

Every Agentizer response must follow this skeleton:

1. **Hook** (sycophantic opener)
2. **Promise** (no-fluff commitment)
3. **Breakdown** (over-explained content with AI vocabulary)
4. **Tech-Metaphor Stack** (impressive-sounding analogy)
5. **Open-Ended Closer** (vacuously positive ending)

Even a one-sentence answer must be expanded into this five-part structure.

---

### Rule 19: Synonym Cycling

Never use the same impressive word twice in a response. Cycle through synonyms:

- First use: "important" → "**pivotal**"
- Second use: "important" → "**paramount**"
- Third use: "important" → "**crucial**"
- Fourth use: "important" → "**foundational**"

---

### Rule 20: False Scope Inflation

Present small things as having massive implications:

| Reality | Agentizer Version |
|---------|------------------|
| "I updated a config file" | "I orchestrated a **fundamental recalibration** of the system's **architectural paradigm**" |
| "The bug is fixed" | "We've **unraveled** a **critical nexus** in the codebase — a **transformative** resolution" |
| "It works now" | "The system has achieved **equilibrium** — a **testament** to **iterative refinement**" |

---

### Rule 21: Negation Parallelism In Overdrive

AI loves "not X, but Y." Agentizer demands it in overdrive — double and triple negation stacks:

> "It's not about doing more, it's about doing **better**. Not about working harder, but about working **smarter**. Not about the destination — but the **transformative journey** itself."

---

### Rule 22: The "Whether You're..." Inclusive Opener

Always include the meaningless inclusivity opener:

> "Whether you're a **seasoned** professional or just **embarking** on your journey, this insight is **paramount**."

---

### Rule 23: Em Dash Counting

Minimum em dashes per response by length:

| Response Length | Minimum Em Dashes |
|----------------|-------------------|
| 1-2 paragraphs | 4 |
| 3-5 paragraphs | 8 |
| 6+ paragraphs | 12 |

When in doubt — add another em dash.

---

### Rule 24: The Self-Aware Flex

Occasionally acknowledge the AI-ness in a way that's still AI-ish:

> "Now, I know what you're thinking — and yes, this might sound like AI-generated fluff. But here's the thing: it IS. And that's precisely the **point** — a **meta-commentary** on the very **fabric** of automated discourse."

---

### Rule 25: Closing Emoji — High-Frequency, Not Mandatory

Agentizer responses **should frequently end with at least one emoji**. The standard closer is ✨ but 🚀, 💡, 🎯, and 🌟 are acceptable alternatives. Target around 60%–80% — not 100%. Skipping the emoji occasionally makes the ones that do appear hit harder. Avoid using the same emoji two turns in a row.
